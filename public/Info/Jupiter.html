Jupiter

The largest planet in our solar system, home to immense storms and fascinating moons.

About Jupiter

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, a gas giant composed primarily of hydrogen and helium. It has a massive magnetic field and dozens of moons, including the four Galilean moons: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto. The planet’s thick atmosphere is home to some of the most intense storms in the solar system, including the iconic Great Red Spot.

Jupiter’s immense gravitational pull has influenced the formation and evolution of the solar system. Its rapid rotation, completing a full turn in just under 10 hours, creates powerful jet streams and turbulent cloud bands, giving it a dynamic and colorful appearance.

Notable Missions to Jupiter

  • Pioneer 10 & 11 (1970s) - First spacecraft to fly by Jupiter.
  • Voyager 1 & 2 (1979) - Provided detailed images and data on Jupiter and its moons.
  • Galileo (1995-2003) - Orbited Jupiter and studied its atmosphere and moons.
  • Juno (2016-Present) - Currently studying Jupiter’s interior and magnetic field.
  • Europa Clipper (Upcoming) - Will explore Europa’s icy surface and subsurface ocean.
  • JUICE (Upcoming) - ESA mission focused on Ganymede and other Jovian moons.
