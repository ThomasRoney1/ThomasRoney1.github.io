Mars

The Red Planet, a world of vast deserts, ancient river valleys, and the next frontier for human exploration.

About Mars

Mars is the fourth planet from the Sun and the second smallest in the solar system. Often called the "Red Planet" due to its iron-rich soil, it has the tallest volcano (Olympus Mons) and the deepest canyon (Valles Marineris) in the solar system. Scientists believe Mars once had liquid water, making it a prime candidate for past life exploration.

Mars has a thin atmosphere composed mostly of carbon dioxide, making it incapable of supporting human life without assistance. However, ongoing missions continue to uncover its mysteries, including the presence of underground ice, seasonal methane emissions, and dust storms that can engulf the entire planet.

Notable Missions to Mars

  • Mariner 4 (1965) - First successful flyby of Mars.
  • Viking 1 & 2 (1976) - First landers to transmit images from the Martian surface.
  • Pathfinder & Sojourner (1997) - First rover on Mars.
  • Spirit & Opportunity (2004) - Twin rovers that explored Mars for years.
  • Curiosity (2012-Present) - Advanced rover studying Martian climate and geology.
  • Perseverance & Ingenuity (2021-Present) - Searching for signs of ancient life and testing powered flight.
