About Voyager

The Voyager program consists of two spacecraft, Voyager 1 and Voyager 2, launched by NASA in 1977. Their primary mission was to explore the outer planets of our solar system, and they have far exceeded expectations, continuing to send back valuable data more than 40 years after their launch.

Voyager 1, the farthest spacecraft from Earth, passed beyond the edge of our solar system in 2012, entering interstellar space. Voyager 2, which took a different trajectory, is the only spacecraft to have visited all four outer planets: Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune. Both spacecraft are now part of NASA's Voyager Interstellar Mission, tasked with studying the environment of interstellar space.

The spacecraft carry the Golden Record, a disc containing sounds and images meant to represent life on Earth to any potential extraterrestrial life that may encounter it. This record includes music, greetings in 55 different languages, and sounds like a kiss and the laughter of children. It serves as a message from humanity, even though the chances of it being discovered are slim.

Despite their distance, the Voyager spacecraft continue to communicate with Earth, sending back valuable data about the solar wind, cosmic rays, and magnetic fields in interstellar space. Their mission has greatly expanded our understanding of the outer regions of our solar system and continues to inspire future space exploration missions.
