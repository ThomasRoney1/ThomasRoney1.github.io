Venus

The Earth's twin — a planet of volcanic landscapes, dense clouds, and extreme greenhouse conditions.

About Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's twin due to its similar size and mass. However, its environment is wildly different: the surface temperature is hot enough to melt lead, and its thick atmosphere traps heat through a runaway greenhouse effect.

Venus’s atmosphere is composed mostly of carbon dioxide, with clouds of sulfuric acid, making it the hottest planet in our solar system. The planet's surface is hidden beneath perpetual cloud cover but radar mapping has revealed vast volcanic plains, mountains, and deformed highlands.

Notable Missions to Venus

  • Venera 7 (1970) - First successful landing on another planet and first data returned from Venus’s surface.
  • Venera 9 (1975) - First images from the surface of Venus.
  • Pioneer Venus (1978) - Orbiter and multiprobe mission that studied Venus’s atmosphere and surface.
  • Magellan (1989) - Used radar to map 98% of Venus’s surface.
  • Akatsuki (2010) - Japanese mission still studying Venus’s weather and atmosphere.
  • VERITAS (Upcoming) - NASA mission planned to map Venus with high resolution radar in the 2030s.
