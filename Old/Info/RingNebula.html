About the Ring Nebula

The Ring Nebula (M57), located in the constellation Lyra about 2,300 light-years away from Earth, is one of the best-known planetary nebulae in the sky. It represents a late stage in a star's life cycle, when a medium-sized star expels its outer layers into space.

The remaining core becomes a white dwarf, illuminating the ejected material with ultraviolet radiation. This radiation excites the gas, causing it to glow in vibrant colors â€” often seen in hues of green, blue, and red depending on the elements present.

Although it appears ring-shaped from our vantage point, the Ring Nebula is actually more like a glowing donut or hourglass structure in 3D. The bright ring is the densest part of the nebula, while fainter material extends outward from both sides.

The Ring Nebula offers astronomers a glimpse into the fate of our own Sun, which will also end its life as a planetary nebula billions of years from now. Studying it helps us understand stellar death, gas dynamics, and chemical enrichment of the galaxy.
