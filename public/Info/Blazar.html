About Blazars

A blazar is a type of active galactic nucleus (AGN) with a relativistic jet pointed almost directly at Earth. These jets, powered by supermassive black holes, emit radiation across the entire electromagnetic spectrum, making blazars some of the brightest and most energetic phenomena in the cosmos.

Blazars are classified into two main types: BL Lacertae objects and flat-spectrum radio quasars (FSRQs). Despite their differences, both exhibit rapid variability, high polarization, and intense emissions due to relativistic beaming.

Observations from telescopes like Fermi Gamma-ray Space Telescope and the Very Long Baseline Array (VLBA) provide insight into the inner workings of blazars, helping scientists understand jet formation, black hole physics, and the evolution of galaxies.

Because of their incredible luminosity and alignment, blazars serve as powerful probes for studying the intergalactic medium and magnetic fields far beyond the Milky Way.
