The heart of the Scorpius constellation, one of the largest and brightest stars in our night sky.

About Antares

Antares is a massive red supergiant star located approximately 550 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Scorpius. It is often referred to as the "Heart of the Scorpion" due to its vivid red appearance and prominent position in the night sky.

With a radius around 680 times that of the Sun and a luminosity roughly 10,000 times greater, Antares is nearing the end of its stellar life. Eventually, it will explode as a spectacular supernova, enriching the galaxy with heavy elements crucial for planet and life formation.

Antares is part of a binary system, accompanied by a much smaller, hotter blue-white companion star. This pairing creates a stunning contrast when viewed through telescopes.

Historically, Antares has held significance across cultures, often associated with war gods and leadership due to its striking color and brightness. Its name means "rival to Ares" (Mars) in Greek, referring to its similar reddish hue.
