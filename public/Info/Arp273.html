Arp 273

A stunning cosmic dance between two gravitationally interacting galaxies.

About Arp 273

Arp 273 is a mesmerizing system of two interacting galaxies located about 300 million light-years away in the constellation Andromeda. The larger of the two galaxies, UGC 1810, exhibits a pronounced tidal distortion, creating a beautiful rose-like spiral structure due to gravitational forces from its smaller companion, UGC 1813.

This galactic interaction triggers bursts of star formation, especially in the smaller galaxy, which appears to have plunged through the larger one’s disk. These interactions are key to understanding how galaxies evolve over time, sometimes merging to form completely new structures.

Arp 273 was cataloged by Halton Arp in his "Atlas of Peculiar Galaxies" — a collection that showcases some of the most fascinating and unusual galaxies observed. The dramatic shapes we see in Arp 273 hint at the intricate gravitational choreography that unfolds over millions of years.

Recent images from the Hubble Space Telescope have captured Arp 273 in stunning detail, highlighting not only the warped disks but also the vivid pockets of active star formation scattered throughout both galaxies.
