  • Sun

    The Sun is a star, a hot ball of glowing gases at the heart of our solar system. Without its energy, life on Earth wouldn’t exist.

  • Mercury

    Mercury is slightly larger than our Moon, cratered and with extreme temperature swings from blazing day to freezing night.

  • Venus

    Venus has a dense, toxic atmosphere and scorching temperatures hot enough to melt lead—thanks to a runaway greenhouse effect.

  • Earth

    Earth is the only world known to support life, with liquid water oceans, an atmosphere, and a protective magnetic field.

  • Mars

    Mars shows evidence of ancient water flows and has the tallest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.

  • Jupiter

    The gas giant Jupiter is the most massive planet, with storms like the Great Red Spot and dozens of moons.

  • Saturn

    Saturn’s iconic rings are made of ice and rock; its volume is 755× Earth’s, and winds blow over 500 m/s.

  • Uranus

    Uranus rotates on its side, with a pale blue color from methane and a faint ring system.

  • Neptune

    Neptune is a deep blue world with supersonic winds and the strongest storms in the solar system.

  • Pluto

    Pluto is a dwarf planet in the Kuiper Belt, known for its heart-shaped Tombaugh Regio and icy surface.
